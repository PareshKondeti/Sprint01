Ingredients(for 2)

  • Chicken Masala: 1 Kg Chicken, 2 tsp Black Cumin, 2 tbsp Ginger Garlic Paste, 2/3 cup Fried Onions, Mint Leaves, 4 spoons Green Coriander, 4 spoons Ghee, Lemon Juice, 2 tbsp Mirchi, 2 tsp Roasted Cumin Powder, 2 tsp Coriander Powder, 1 tsp Garam Masala, 1/2 tsp Turmeric, Salt, 500 ml Curd, 2 Biryani Leaf, 4 Cardamoms, 2" Cinnamon Stick
  • Biryani: 4 liters Water, 10 Cardamoms, 2" Cinnamon Stick, 12 Cloves, 2 tsp Black Cumin, 2 Biryani Leaf, 2 tbsp Ginger Garlic Paste, 4 tbsp Salt, 3 cups Basmati Rice (225 gms)
  • Biryani Dum: 4 tsp Coriander, 1/2 cup Ghee, 1/2 cup Fried Onions, Saffron (Soaked in hot Water)

Instructions

  • Add all spices to Â½ kilo tender chicken and coat the pieces well.
  • Marinate for two hours or refrigerate overnight for juicier results.
  • If left overnight, the flavors intensify, making the chicken tender and flavorful. If without a refrigerator, leave it outside.
  • Boil 2 liters of water and add all the biryani ingredients.
  • Once the water boils, add the basmati rice (soaked for an hour) and cook on high flame until 70% done. The rice should be slightly brittle but cooked, indicating it has reached the desired doneness.

  • Add the marinated chicken to a thick-bottomed vessel.
  • Strain the cooked basmati rice and biryani masalas, layering them over the chicken.
  • Sprinkle green coriander, garam masala, ghee, and saffron water over the rice.
  • Cover with tissues or a banana leaf and seal to retain the steam.
  • Cook (dum) the rice for 8 minutes on medium flame, followed by 7 minutes on low flame.
  • After switching off the stove, let it rest for 15 minutes.
  • Gently turn the biryani with a flat ladle from the bottom before serving.
